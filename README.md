AI Agents Security Risk Assessment – ABSA Scenario (Case Study)
Overview

This project presents a cybersecurity and risk assessment of AI agents deployed within a financial services environment inspired by ABSA operations in South Africa that was done as part of the Southern Africa - Netherlands Cyber Security School (SANCS ) DataSync-Cybersecurity-Challenge.

As financial institutions adopt AI to automate processes and enhance decision-making, new security risks emerge. This case study evaluates how poorly governed AI agents can introduce vulnerabilities, particularly in highly regulated environments like banking.

Objectives
Identify security failures in AI agent deployment within a banking environment
Analyse root causes of vulnerabilities affecting financial systems
Assess and prioritise risks based on regulatory and operational impact
Develop a practical 90-day remediation plan aligned with financial sector standards
Scenario Context (ABSA-Inspired)

A large financial institution deployed multiple AI agents across key operations, including:

Customer service automation
Fraud detection support
Internal workflow automation
Data analysis and reporting systems

Following deployment, the organisation experienced several security incidents, exposing weaknesses in governance, access control, and monitoring.

Key Security Failures Identified
Excessive system access: AI agents were granted broad permissions beyond operational needs
Lack of governance frameworks: No clear policies guiding AI deployment and usage
Insufficient monitoring: Limited tracking of AI agent decisions and system interactions
Weak data protection controls: Sensitive financial data exposed to potential misuse
No pre-deployment risk assessment: AI agents deployed without proper security evaluation
Risk Analysis (Financial Sector Focus)

High Risk
Exposure of sensitive customer financial data (POPIA compliance risk)
Unauthorized transactions or system manipulation
Regulatory penalties and reputational damage
Medium Risk
Incorrect fraud detection decisions impacting customers
Operational inefficiencies and service disruptions
Low Risk
Minor automation errors with limited business impact
Reduced system performance visibility

90-Day Remediation Plan

0–30 Days (Immediate Risk Reduction)
Restrict AI agent permissions using role-based access control (RBAC)
Conduct a full audit of all AI agents and their system interactions
Disable high-risk functionalities and isolate vulnerable systems
Implement logging and monitoring of all AI agent activities

30–60 Days (Security Strengthening)
Develop and enforce AI governance and security policies
Introduce regular security testing (vulnerability assessments)
Strengthen data protection (encryption and access controls)
Train employees on AI risks and cybersecurity best practices

60–90 Days (Long-Term Governance)
Implement continuous monitoring and incident response systems
Align AI usage with financial regulations (e.g., POPIA compliance)
Establish secure AI deployment frameworks
Integrate AI risk management into overall IT governance strategy

Skills Demonstrated
Cybersecurity risk assessment in financial services
AI governance and regulatory awareness (POPIA context)
Risk prioritisation and mitigation planning
Analytical and problem-solving skills
Translating technical risks into business impact

Project Value
This project demonstrates the importance of securing AI systems in banking environments. It highlights how poor governance can expose institutions to serious financial and regulatory risks, and how structured remediation can ensure safe and responsible AI adoption.
